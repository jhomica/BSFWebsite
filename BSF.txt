   <h2 class="secA">About</h2>
   <p class="secA">The <b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> (BSF) is a public school located in <b>Orion, Bataan</b>.
It was established in <b>1971</b> through <b>Republic Act 4222</b>, enacted    by the <b>5th Congress on June 19, 1965</b>,
under the supervision of the <b>Director of Vocational Education</b>. The school is <i>located</i> in <b>Barangay Daan Bago</b>, 
with a total land area of <i>14.8 hectares for the fishpond</i>. <i>Laboratory, 2.08 hectares for mangrove forest, and 6 hectares of 
reservation area for the fishing laboratory</i>.
<br>
In <b>1971</b>, the <b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> opened its gates for its first year of operation. It offered a 
<b>Secondary Technical Vocational Program</b> with specialization in <b>Fish Capture</b>, <b>Fish Culture</b> and
<b>Fish Preservation</b>. BSF then expanded its curricular program in <b>1975</b> to offer a three-year <b>Post Secondary Diploma
in Fisheries Technology</b> (DFT) with specialization in <b>Marine Fisheries</b>, <b>Aquaculture</b>, and <b>Fish Processing</b>.
<br>
However, in <b>1994</b>, <i>BSF was placed under</i> <b>TESDA</b> (Technical Education and Skills Development Authority) supervision
through <b>R.A. 7796</b>. During this period, BSF gained accreditation for the offering of <b>Basic Seamanship</b>, 
<b>Garments Technology</b> and <b>One-Year Computer Literacy Course</b> in addition to the <i>FBPS</i>, <i>FPST</i>, and 
<i>DFT</i>. In <b>1998</b>, <b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> opted to return to the <i>supervision</i> of the <b>Department
of Education, Culture, and Sports, Department of Education</b> (DepEd). BSF also started to conduct
<b>TESDA</b>-<i>supervised trade tests for graduating students in Garment Technology.</i>
<br>  
<b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> gained <i>accreditation</i> in <b>1999</b> as an assessment center for 
<b>Food Trades</b> and <b>Computer Encoding</b> for graduates of <b>FBPS</b> and the <b>One-Year Computer Literacy Program</b>. In <b>2007</b>, BSF was 
identified as thepilot school for the <b>implementation of the Strengthened Technical-Vocational Education Program</b> 
(STEVEP) of the Department of Education. It also started to conduct TESDA. <b>Supervised Competency Assessment</b> 
for its <i>graduating high schools</i>.
<br>
In addition, in <b>2008</b>, BSF contracted a <b>Memorandum of Agreement</b> with <b>Bataan Peninsula State University </b>(BPSU) for the<i>College</i> consortium to offer <b>Bachelor of Science</b> in Fisheries and Associate in <b>Computer Technology programs</b>. Which then 
in <i>2010</i>, <b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> was <i>identified</i> as a <b>National Benchmarking School for Department Heads of Fishery Schools</b> throughout the <b>Philippines</b>.  
<br>
Further, in <i>2012</i>, <b>BSF</b> was identified as a <b>Modelling Tech-Voc School in Fisheries</b> for the <i>Grade 11</i> and <i>Grade 
12 Senior High School</i> of the <b>K-12 Program</b>. There are only 14 schools in the country that participated in the said Modelling Program 
of which BSF is one. In <i>2013</i>, <b>Bataan School of Fisheries</b> was accredited as a <b>Training and Assessment Center in Fish/Food Processing NCII, Aquaculture NCII, Aquaculture INC II, Fish Capture NCII, Dressmaking NCII and Food & Beverages NCII</b>.  
At present, the school has <i>2309 students</i> in both the Junior High School <i>76</i> and Senior High School <i>22</i> with <i>98</i> teachers.
 Under the supervision of <b>Dr. Marijoy B. Mendoza School Principal III, and Arvin N. De Dios, Assistant School Principal II.</b></p>

<h2 class="secB">DepEd Mission</h2>
<p class="secB">To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:
Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment.
Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.
Administrators and staff, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen.
Family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.</P>
